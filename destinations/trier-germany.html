Why families love it

Germany's oldest city blends Roman ruins with a compact old town that is easy for families to explore. Kids can walk the Porta Nigra, climb the amphitheater, and still have time for a riverside stroll along the Mosel.

  • Porta Nigra and Simeonstiftplatz
  • Trier Cathedral and Liebfrauenkirche
  • Basilica of Constantine
  • Kaiserthermen or Roman Amphitheatre
  • Mosel river promenade

Suggested 1 day plan

Morning

Start at Porta Nigra, then walk to the Cathedral and Liebfrauenkirche.

Midday

Lunch around Hauptmarkt, then a quick stop at Karl Marx House or the Toy Museum.

Afternoon

Visit the Basilica of Constantine and Kaiserthermen, then head to the amphitheater.

Late afternoon

Finish with a Mosel river promenade stroll and an easy return to KMC.

Getting there

Trier is easy by car or regional train. From Trier Hbf, the old town is a 15 to 20 minute walk or a short bus ride. If driving, use City Parkhaus or Parkhaus Hauptmarkt and walk into the center.

Family travel tips

  • Park at a central garage to keep the day walkable.
  • Bring hats and sunscreen for outdoor Roman sites.
  • Use the Hauptmarkt area for a simple lunch stop.
